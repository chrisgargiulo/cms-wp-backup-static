General Advice for Design Students<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

Choosing a project is a challenge faced by many design students. Here are a few general tips and questions you can ask yourself.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

General Tips, Advice, & Questions<\/h3>\n\n\n\n
  1. Be smart and do your research<\/strong>, then make an informed decision.<\/em>
    • What precedent projects exist that inspire you?<\/li>
    • What past projects were successful? Which were not? Why? <\/li><\/ul><\/li>
    • Consider the scope<\/strong> and choose a project that is realistically achievable within your timeline.<\/em>
      • How large is this project? Can I make it smaller? <\/li>
      • Sample range of different scopes for UI projects:
        • Small: a Single Page Client Site<\/strong> (e.g. a one page site for a local small business, event, or PSA campaign)
          • Examples:
            • Michael Harrell’s Extended Play Bar<\/a> website<\/li>
            • Reyn Suzuki’s CanPlan<\/a> website<\/li>
            • Stephan Bradley’s Eat The Stree<\/a>t website<\/li><\/ul><\/li><\/ul><\/li>
            • Small-Medium: an Individual Client Site<\/strong> (e.g. a 2-5 page site for a local visual artist, photographer, musician, politician, or cultural\/historical icon)
              • Examples:
                • Kimi Lung’s On The Street<\/a> website for photographer Bill Cunningham<\/li>
                • Nhi Nguyen’s Yayoi Kusama<\/a> website<\/li><\/ul><\/li><\/ul><\/li>
                • Medium: a Small Business Client Site<\/strong> (e.g. a 2-10 page site for a local restaurant, business, non-profit organization, or startup)
                  • Examples:
                    • Liana Young’s Kalihi Pet Center<\/a> website<\/li>
                    • Yoon’s Kakuida<\/a> website<\/li><\/ul><\/li><\/ul><\/li>
                    • Medium: a Unique Concept Site<\/strong> or Fictional Product Site<\/strong> (e.g. an art project, an open-source project, a learning resource, a new custom product)
                      • Examples:
                        • David Efhan’s Type Always<\/a> website<\/li>
                        • David Efhan’s UltraType<\/a> website<\/li>
                        • Liana Young’s Prime<\/a> app<\/li><\/ul><\/li><\/ul><\/li><\/ul><\/li><\/ul><\/li>
                        • Envision your future \u201cideal\u201d portfolio<\/strong> and choose a project that will fit nicely in your eventual portfolio.<\/em>
                          • Is there a particular visual style that you are interested in exploring that is currently missing from your portfolio?<\/li>
                          • Is there a medium that you have yet to explore and would like to? <\/li><\/ul><\/li>
                          • Consider breadth vs. depth<\/strong> and choose a project that matches your goals as a student.<\/em>
                            • Should I do another<\/em> project (like my other ones), to demonstrate a niche specialty\/focus by going deeper?<\/li>
                            • Should I do something totally different<\/em> from my other projects, to broaden my portfolio to be more well-rounded? <\/li><\/ul><\/li>
                            • Envision of your future \u201cideal\u201d career<\/strong> and choose a project that aligns with your career goals?<\/em>
                              • What type of client projects do you hope to work on after you graduate?<\/li>
                              • What kind of designer do you hope to be? A generalist or specialist? <\/li><\/ul><\/li>
                              • Follow your heart<\/strong> and be passionate!<\/em>
                                • Are you totally excited to work on this project? If not, then choose another. If so, then go for it! <\/li><\/ul><\/li><\/ol>\n\n\n\n

                                  Make a Wish (List)<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

                                  Write down your personal goals and prioritize them<\/em><\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                  1. Technology Related:
                                    • Technologies to use\/learn\/test:div
                                      • Github (cloud-based version control & code sharing)<\/li>
                                      • WordPress (custom theme from scratch or a child theme)<\/li>
                                      • Web App Prototype (with Hi-fidelity UI design and videos\/animated gifs)<\/li>
                                      • Simple Native Web App (using Phonegap Build)<\/li>
                                      • CSS3 Animation (test out different techniques)<\/li>
                                      • My Own Custom Framework (similar to Site Base, Bootstrap, Foundation, Skeleton)<\/li>
                                      • Responsive, Retina-ready graphics\/imagery (including svg\u2019s)<\/li><\/ul><\/li><\/ul>
                                        • Technologies to do more projects with:
                                          • CSS preprocessors (ie SASS)<\/li>
                                          • Static Site Generators (SSG’s like Astro, Jekyll, Eleventy, Hugo, etc.)<\/li>
                                          • Vanilla Javascript (no JQuery)<\/li>
                                          • Custom Grid Systems (and advanced grid layouts w\/ flexbox and\/or css grid))<\/li>
                                          • Slider\/Carousel\/Slideshow<\/li>
                                          • Better Web Fonts\/Typography<\/li>
                                          • Single Page Site w\/ Vertical Animation<\/li>
                                          • Third-Party Mobile Frameworks (Jquery Mobile, Framework 7, Ionic, etc)<\/li>
                                          • Front-end Frameworks (e.g. React, Vue, & Angular)<\/li><\/ul><\/li><\/ul><\/li>
                                          • Other things that you might like to integrate:
                                            • A Portfolio project with a \u201cSimple\u201d (less is more) minimalist UI (ie. flat and material)<\/li>
                                            • Good use of the Grid (ie. The Magazine Look, Print Design Influence)<\/li>
                                            • Rich, Strong Typography<\/li>
                                            • Multiple Stacked Hero sections (e.g. \u201cPosters\u201d)<\/li>
                                            • 1-2-3\/Step-by-step Walk-through\/Instructions<\/li>
                                            • Speaking Navigation<\/li>
                                            • 3D\/Depth<\/li>
                                            • Custom Icons & Visual Cues (ie. badges\/starbursts, etc.)<\/li>
                                            • Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, & YouTube Integration<\/li>
                                            • Interesting backgrounds (ie. photos? gradients? patterns? stripes? etc.)<\/li>
                                            • More White Space<\/li>
                                            • Large, stylized footer <\/li><\/ul><\/li><\/ol>\n\n\n\n
                                              <\/div>\n\n\n\n

                                              Think Conceptually, Award-Winning, and BIG (take risks & have fun)!<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

                                              Do not choose a safe client project that is too simple or convenient. For example, even though your Uncle needs a website for his auto parts store, do not choose this unless you are psyched<\/em> to work on his auto parts site and have clever plans to make it interesting, unique, and stand out. Instead, choose something with the potential to be conceptually<\/em> strong, with the opportunity to create compelling visuals (animation!) and clever copywriting (strong typography!). If your goal is do win a local award, then consider choosing something local (e.g. a local business to redesign or local PSA campaign) with the potential to stand out both visually and conceptually. Take a close look at previous award winning projects for inspiration.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                              • Round-up of Conceptual Tips:
                                                • Consider Going Local (local businesses, artists, events, musicians)<\/li>
                                                • Consider Doing Good via a PSA (in support for a local cause, a local non-profit, etc.)<\/li>
                                                • Consider Solving a Design Problem (be creative, develop a solution for a known problem, and dream big!)<\/li><\/ul><\/li><\/ul>\n\n\n\n
                                                  <\/div>\n\n\n\n

                                                  Conclusion<\/h3>\n\n\n\n

                                                  Choosing a school project can be challenge. But like all design problems, you can find a solution by being an informed designer and think critically about your chosen project.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
